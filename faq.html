Our Process

How does GoldRock's cash home buying process work? ⌄
Pretty simple, actually. You fill out the form, Tyler or Riley calls you, we schedule a walkthrough, and within 24-48 hours of seeing the property we put a real cash offer in your hands. No listings. No showings. Just a straight conversation, a fair number, and a closing date you actually control. That's it.
What happens after I submit the form? ⌄
Tyler or Riley will reach out to you — fast. We'll have a quick, no-pressure conversation about your situation, your property, and what you're looking for. If it makes sense, we'll schedule a walkthrough. If it doesn't, we'll tell you honestly. No games.
Do I have to clean or fix up the house before you visit? ⌄
No. Seriously. Leave it exactly as it is. Full of stuff, dirty, damaged — we don't care. We're not walking through to judge your home. We're walking through to make you an offer. Leave whatever you don't want and walk out.
Do I need a real estate agent to work with GoldRock? ⌄
Nope. You deal directly with Tyler and Riley — that's the whole point. If you already have an agent and want to involve them, that's fine too — we work well with agents.
Is there any cost or obligation to request an offer? ⌄
Zero obligation. Requesting an offer is free and commits you to absolutely nothing. You can say yes, say no, or take some time to think about it. We're not going to pressure you into a decision.

Cash Offers

How is GoldRock's cash offer calculated? ⌄
We look at real Colorado market data, recent comparable sales in your specific neighborhood, and the actual condition of your home. We also factor in rehab and repair costs, holding costs while we own the property, market fluctuations, commissions we pay on the sell side, buyer concessions when we sell, and the risk we're taking on buying as-is. We're not throwing out a fantasy number to get you under contract. We do our homework before we walk through the door — so when we make an offer, it's a number we stand behind.
Will GoldRock's offer be lower than market value? ⌄
It might be. And we'll be upfront about that. But here's what you need to factor in: no agent commissions (5–6%), no repair costs, no staging, no months of carrying costs, no deal falling through at the last second. When you add all that up, many sellers walk away with more — or very close to the same — as they would have through a traditional sale. We'd rather give you an honest number than an inflated one we can't back up.
How fast will I receive a cash offer? ⌄
We deliver a cash offer within 24-48 hours of the walkthrough. Not 24-48 hours from when you fill out the form — 24-48 hours after we see the property in person. We do our homework before we put a number in your hands.
Are there any fees, commissions, or hidden costs? ⌄
None. Zero. We don't charge commissions. No hidden fees or service costs. We're not going to sneak closing costs into the fine print either.
What if I get a higher offer from someone else? ⌄
You should absolutely consider it — but read the contract carefully. Is it a Colorado Real Estate Commission approved contract? Is there an inspection period they can use to back out or renegotiate? Is the closing date specified? Is the earnest money held by a title company? If the answer to any of those is no, that "higher" offer may not be worth the paper it's written on. A real offer from GoldRock beats a fake high number every time.

Contracts & Paperwork

What kind of contract does GoldRock use? ⌄
We use Colorado Real Estate Commission approved contracts — the same contracts used by licensed Colorado real estate agents and attorneys. Not a self-written document full of vague language. A real, state-approved contract you can hand to your attorney and it'll hold up.
What should I watch out for with other cash buyers' contracts? ⌄
Most cash buyers in Colorado are NOT using Colorado Real Estate Commission approved contracts. They write their own — filled with vague language, missing closing dates, and no real earnest money deadlines. Watch for: no specified closing date, broad inspection periods they can use to renegotiate, assignment clauses that let them sell your contract to a third party, and minimal earnest money held by themselves rather than a title company. If it's not a CREC approved contract — walk away.
Does GoldRock have an inspection period? ⌄
No. Here's the truth nobody tells you: a real investor doesn't need an inspection period. If someone is buying your home with cash, as-is, they've already done their homework before making the offer. An inspection period in a cash offer is a red flag — it means they're not a real investor. We walk the property, we assess it ourselves, and we make a decision — no inspection contingency, no escape hatch, no way out hiding in the fine print.
Where is the earnest money held? ⌄
Always with a licensed Colorado title company — never in our pocket. It goes straight into escrow under clear, defined conditions that protect you as much as they protect us. You know exactly what happens to it, when it releases, and under what circumstances.
Can I have an attorney review the contract? ⌄
Absolutely — and we encourage it. Because we use CREC approved contracts, any real estate attorney in Colorado will recognize it immediately. We have nothing to hide. The contract is designed to protect both parties, not just us.

Property Conditions

Will GoldRock buy my home as-is, even if it needs major repairs? ⌄
Yes. And we mean it. Fire damage, foundation issues, roof falling in, mold, hoarder situation, hasn't been cleaned in years — we don't care. We buy the home exactly as it sits. You don't spend a dollar, lift a finger, or make a single phone call to a contractor before we close.
Do you buy homes with mold, fire damage, or flood damage? ⌄
Yes to all three. Mold doesn't scare us — you don't need to remediate anything before closing. Fire damage, whether partial or total, we assess ourselves and make a real offer. Water and flood damage, rot, structural issues from water — we've seen it all and we still buy.
Do you buy hoarder homes? ⌄
Yes. No judgment, no cleanup required. Leave everything. Take only what you want. We handle everything after closing. We've bought homes in every condition imaginable — a hoarder situation doesn't change our offer process one bit.
What if there are squatters or tenants in the property? ⌄
We've dealt with occupied properties in every shape and form — bad tenants, mid-eviction situations, squatters, unauthorized occupants. It's not a dealbreaker. We'll talk through the situation and figure out the best path forward.
Do you buy homes with code violations or city citations? ⌄
Yes. Health and safety violations, city orders, open permits — we buy properties with these issues. We understand how to navigate them and they won't kill the deal.

Timeline & Closing

How fast can GoldRock close on my Colorado home? ⌄
As fast as 7 to 14 days — or as slow as you need. We work on your timeline, not ours. We have the cash. When you're ready, we close. It's genuinely that simple.
Who picks the closing date? ⌄
You do. Need to close in 7 days because you're relocating? Done. Need 60 days to find your next place and get your ducks in a row? Also done. You tell us the date, we show up. No buyer asking for a 2-week extension because their dog got sick. You're in control — and that's exactly how it should be.
Is the closing date in the contract? ⌄
Yes. Always. Our contracts have a defined closing date — not "to be determined," not "within a reasonable timeframe," not language so vague a lawyer would laugh. A real date. Written down. And we show up. We already have the cash. We just need the date.
What happens at closing? ⌄
We close through a licensed Colorado title company. You sign the paperwork, the title company handles the transfer, and you receive your funds — typically by wire transfer the same day or next business day. The whole process is handled professionally, transparently, and without surprises.

Situations We Help With

What types of situations does GoldRock help Colorado homeowners with? ⌄
We help Colorado homeowners in virtually every difficult situation — here's what we specialize in:

Pre-foreclosure, behind on mortgage payments, forbearance ending, loan default, tax delinquency or tax liens, HOA liens, bankruptcy (Chapter 7 or 13), judgments or creditor liens, wage garnishment pressure, and hard money loans coming due.

Divorce or separation, inherited property (probate), death in the family, guardianship or conservatorship situations, code violations, and city citations.

Major repairs needed (roof, foundation, HVAC, sewer), fire damage, flood or water damage, mold, structural problems, vacant and deteriorating property, hoarder houses, vandalized properties, and squatters.

Job relocation, downsizing, upsizing, retirement transition, health issues or medical bills, moving into assisted living, bad tenants or eviction situations.

If your situation isn't listed here — reach out anyway. If there's a way to help you, GoldRock will find it.
Can you help if I'm in active foreclosure or facing a foreclosure auction? ⌄
Yes — but time matters enormously. If you have a foreclosure auction scheduled, reach out immediately. The sooner we can get in front of your property and make an offer, the more options you have. Don't wait until the week of the auction. Call now.
Can you buy a property that's in probate? ⌄
Yes. We understand the legal complexity of probate situations and work with you and your attorney to navigate the process. We make it as simple and respectful as possible during what is often an already difficult time.
My listing expired and didn't sell. Can GoldRock still help? ⌄
Absolutely. A home that sat on the MLS and didn't sell is often exactly the type we buy. It's not that the home can't be sold — it's that it couldn't be sold the traditional way. That's our specialty. Reach out and let's take a look.
