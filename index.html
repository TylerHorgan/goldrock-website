THE HONEST TRUTH

GoldRock vs. The Other Guys

Not all cash buyers are the same. Some are honest, fair, and actually have the cash. Others... aren't. Here's what you need to know before you sign anything.

THE OTHER GUYS

What to Watch Out For

⚠

They Hand You a Contract They Wrote Themselves

Most cash buyers in Colorado are NOT using Colorado Real Estate Commission approved contracts. They write their own — filled with vague language, missing closing dates, and no real earnest money deadlines. That contract protects THEM, not you. If something goes sideways, you have almost zero legal recourse. Read the fine print. Or better yet, ask them who approved it.

⚠

The "Too Good to Be True" Offer — Because It Is

They walk in, look at your home, and throw out a number that makes your jaw drop. $50,000 over what anyone else offered. You think you found a unicorn. You didn't. That inflated offer is bait — designed to get you under contract and get your guard down. Once you're locked in, the real game starts.

⚠

The Inspection Period Is Their Escape Hatch — Not Yours

Here's the truth nobody tells you: a real investor doesn't need an inspection period. If someone is buying your home with cash, as-is, they've already done their homework before making the offer. An inspection period in a cash offer is a 🚩 red flag — it means they're not a real investor. They're a wholesaler, a middleman, or someone who doesn't actually have the money and is using your contract as a fishing pole. Walk away.

⚠

They Tell You Everything You Want to Hear

No repairs. Fast close. Top dollar. Smooth process. They say all the right things on the phone — because they've said them a thousand times before. What they don't tell you is that none of it is in the contract. Verbal promises mean nothing in real estate. If it's not written down and legally binding, it doesn't exist.

⚠

No Closing Date Means No Commitment — Just Control Over You

Their contracts conveniently leave the closing date open-ended or undefined. That's not an oversight — it's leverage. It lets them drag the deal out for weeks or months while they line up financing, find another buyer, or wait for a better opportunity. You're stuck. Can't accept other offers. Can't move forward. Just waiting on someone who made you a promise they were never legally required to keep.

⚠

Earnest Money That Disappears Into Thin Air

In a legitimate real estate transaction, earnest money is held by a neutral third party — a title company or attorney — and has clear release conditions. Shady cash buyers often put in minimal earnest money, hold it themselves, or write contracts where they can get it back for almost any reason. If they walk, you get nothing. If you walk, good luck.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does GoldRock's cash home buying process work in Colorado? ⌄
Pretty simple, actually. You fill out the form, Tyler or Riley calls you, we schedule a walkthrough, and within 24-48 hours of seeing the property we put a real cash offer in your hands. No listings. How fast can GoldRock close on my Colorado home? ⌄
As fast as 7 days — or as slow as you need. We work on your timeline, not ours. We have the cash. When you're ready, we close. It's genuinely that simple.
Will GoldRock buy my home as-is, even if it needs repairs? ⌄
Yes. And we mean it. Fire damage, foundation issues, roof falling in, mold, hoarder situation, hasn't been cleaned in years — we don't care. We buy the home exactly as it sits. You don't spend a dollar, make a single phone call to a contractor, or lift a finger before closing. Just leave what you don't want and walk out.
Are there any fees, commissions, or hidden costs? ⌄
None. Zero. We don't charge commissions. No hidden fees or service costs. We're not going to sneak closing costs into the fine print either.
How is GoldRock's cash offer calculated? ⌄
We look at real Colorado market data, recent comparable sales in your specific neighborhood, and the actual condition of your home. We also factor in rehab and repair costs, holding costs while we own the property, market fluctuations, commissions we pay on the sell side, buyer concessions when we sell, and the risk we're taking on buying as-is. We're not throwing out a fantasy number to get you under contract. We do our homework before we walk through the door — so when we make an offer, it's a number we stand behind.

Pre-foreclosure, behind on mortgage payments, forbearance ending, loan default, tax delinquency or tax liens, HOA liens, bankruptcy (Chapter 7 or 13), judgments or creditor liens, wage garnishment pressure, and hard money loans coming due.

Major repairs needed (roof, foundation, HVAC, sewer), fire damage, flood or water damage, mold, structural problems, vacant and deteriorating property, hoarder houses, vandalized properties, and squatters.

Job relocation, downsizing, upsizing, retirement transition, health issues or medical bills, moving into assisted living, bad tenants or eviction situations.

Major unexpected repairs, vacant properties, landlord fatigue, partnership disputes, out-of-state owner issues, and portfolio liquidation.

House sitting on market with no offers, expired or withdrawn listings, repeated price reductions, needing to sell before buying another home, and closing deadline crunches.

Facing foreclosure auction within days or weeks, abandoning property, severe financial hardship, property becoming a liability (insurance dropped or property deemed unsafe), and the very common divorce plus foreclosure combination.
